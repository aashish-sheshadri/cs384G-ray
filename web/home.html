One of the characteristics of art is the exploration of expression. For example, dipole painting is a technique based on the juxtaposing of two conflicting ideas in order to create meaning. Moreover, this technique usually yields interesting composition. Inspired by this technique, we explore the use of photo realistic and non-photo realistic effects in order to enhance ray tracing.

List of Accomplishments:

  • A non photo realistic illumination model
  • Edge rendering algorithm
  • Texture Mapping
  • Bump Mapping
  • Depth of Field
  • Adaptive Sampling

Photo Realistic Effects

Texture Mapping

In order for the audience to determine a rendered object to be real, one must be capable of accurately depicting the intricate characteristics of the object in great detail. The first step to achieve the goal is to seamlessly wrap images of such complex features to simple objects such as cubes and spheres.
